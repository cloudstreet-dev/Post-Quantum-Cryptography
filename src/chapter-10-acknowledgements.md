The NIST Post-Quantum Cryptography team ran an eight-year open competition with rigorous public review. The quality of the resulting standards reflects that process. The cryptographic community — researchers who submitted algorithms, cryptanalysts who broke them, and reviewers who improved the survivors — did the actual work that this book describes.

The Open Quantum Safe project provides the reference implementations and benchmarking infrastructure that make practical PQC deployment possible. Without accessible, well-tested library code, standardisation would matter considerably less.
